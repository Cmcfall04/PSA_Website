**Call to Action Button:**
"See What They're Not Showing You"

## Section 1: The Problem

**Heading:** The Water Crisis Is Already Here — And It's Closer Than You Think

**Body:**
Most people think of water scarcity as someone else's problem. A drought in Arizona. A "Day Zero" warning in Cape Town. But the reality is much closer to home.

- Nearly 30 million Americans are living in areas with limited water supplies. *(USGS, January 2025)*
- Roughly 53% of the nation's aquifers are drying up as global water systems confront warming. *(Grist, January 2025)*
- Nearly half of the 204 freshwater basins in the U.S. may not be able to meet monthly water demand by 2071. *(U.S. Forest Service / Colorado State University)*
- Florida is currently experiencing a Level 2 drought — an 11-inch rainfall deficit — affecting both the environment and local communities.

Fresh water makes up only 2.5% of all water on Earth. We are using it faster than it can be replenished.

**IMAGE SUGGESTION:**
A map of the United States highlighting drought-affected regions, or a close-up photo of a dried, cracked Florida landscape or low water reservoir. This grounds the statistics visually and makes the problem feel real and local rather than abstract.

## Section 2: The Hidden Cost of Luxury Water Use

**Heading:** What Your Feed Isn't Showing You

**Body:**
Social media is full of water. Pool content. Golf course aesthetics. WaterTok. But none of it shows you where that water comes from — or what it costs.

### Golf Courses

**IMAGE SUGGESTION:**
A wide aerial shot of a bright green golf course surrounded by dry, brown land. The contrast between the maintained green and the dry environment around it makes the point without needing much text.

- A typical American golf course uses around **312,000 gallons of water per day** to maintain its greens. *(Audubon International / NPR)*
- In arid regions like Palm Springs, that number exceeds **1 million gallons per day** — equal to what a family of four uses in four years. *(NPR)*
- Golf course irrigation across the U.S. totals approximately **2.08 billion gallons per day**. *(USGA)*

### Social Media Trends

**IMAGE SUGGESTION:**
A screenshot-style graphic designed to look like a TikTok or Instagram post, showing a WaterTok-style video with a stat overlaid showing how many gallons were wasted. This keeps the section visually native to the platforms your audience uses and makes the critique feel immediate.

- Trends like WaterTok normalize the consumption and waste of water as entertainment, with no discussion of environmental cost.
- Platforms reward aspirational, consequence-free content — the reality of scarcity is rarely visible or viral.

### The Bigger Picture
Water is not just being used. It is being used for aesthetics, entertainment, and trends — while millions of Americans face shrinking supplies.

## Section 3: Why It Matters Here in Tampa

**Heading:** This Is a Tampa Problem Too

**Body:**
Florida may feel like a wet state, but it is not immune. The same water being used to keep golf courses green and water parks running is drawn from the same stressed systems that supply our drinking water.

- Florida is one of the top four states for total water withdrawals in the U.S., alongside California, Texas, and Idaho. *(USGS)*
- The state is currently under a Level 2 drought.
- Tampa sits in a region where the demand for water is growing while local supplies are under increasing pressure.

If we cannot address the problem locally, we cannot begin to address it globally.

**IMAGE SUGGESTION:**
A recognizable Tampa or Florida local image — ideally a side-by-side of a familiar Tampa landmark or green space next to a visual of drought or low water levels. A photo of the University of Tampa campus with its irrigation systems or green lawns visible would also work well. Keeping it local makes the audience feel personally connected to the issue.

## Section 4: What You Can Do — The Call to Action

**Heading:** You Have More Power Than You Think

**Body:**
This is not about guilt. It is about awareness and action. Here is how you can start:

### On Social Media
- When you see luxury water content, ask: *Where does this water come from?*
- Share our campaign posts and use **#[YourHashtagHere]**
- Reframe the narrative — post about water conservation in a way that actually reaches people

### On Campus (University of Tampa)
- Push for transparent water usage reporting from UT administration
- Engage student athletic organizations about the water footprint of maintaining fields and courses
- Advocate for campus-wide water conservation initiatives

### In Your Daily Life
- Reduce lawn and landscaping water waste
- Support businesses and organizations that practice water recycling
- Stay informed — follow local drought conditions and water policy updates

**IMAGE SUGGESTION:**
A candid photo of students on the UT campus engaging with their phones or with each other — something that feels relatable and active rather than preachy. A photo your group takes yourselves on campus would add the most authenticity and keeps it local and personal.

## Section 5: Our Partners

**Heading:** Who's Already Doing It Right

**Body:**
Change is possible. Walt Disney World Resort — one of the largest water users in Florida — uses reclaimed water for 80% of its irrigation needs and 30% of its overall water needs. If a resort the size of a small city can do it, so can we.

We are also working with **Tampa Terrance**, a local Tampa Bay influencer, to bring this message to the community in a way that is honest, relatable, and actually worth watching.

**IMAGE SUGGESTION:**
Two images side by side. One of Tampa Terrance pulled from his public social media with credit, and one representing Disney's sustainability efforts such as their solar farm or a green space at the resort. This section is about credibility, so the images should feel trustworthy and recognizable.

## Section 6: About the Campaign

**Heading:** About This Campaign

**Body:**
This campaign was created by students at the University of Tampa as part of a Water Justice media project. Our goal is simple: make visible what luxury water culture keeps hidden, and inspire real action starting right here in our own community.

We are not asking you to stop having fun. We are asking you to think about what fun actually costs — and whether we can afford to keep paying that price.

**IMAGE SUGGESTION:**
A group photo of your campaign team taken on the UT campus. This humanizes the campaign and shows it is driven by real students from the community. Authenticity matters especially for a Gen Z audience, and nothing is more authentic than putting real faces to the message.
